## The idea that shouldn't have worked

Have you ever wondered what it would be like to walk home from work instead of taking public transport?

Well, I did. And I did it.

My "workplace" — or as I like to call it, my second home — is SAFTI MI, all the way in the west end of Singapore, near Joo Koon MRT. On a regular day, it takes around two hours just to commute there. So naturally, the logical thing to do was to walk back.

On one fateful Thursday morning, I got out of wingline at 5AM, fuelled up on the SAFTI MI high-protein breakfast, armed myself with five packets of oat sachets, and set off.

## No maps. No problem.

I set myself one rule: no Google Maps.

Instead, I navigated by following the MRT lines overhead and weaving through Singapore's network of Park Connector Networks (PCNs). There's something oddly satisfying about trusting your instincts through a city you think you know — and then realising you don't.

The PCNs are genuinely underrated. Green, quiet, and completely free of traffic — they're the kind of paths that make you forget you're in one of the world's most dense cities.

## Four reservoirs deep

The route ended up taking me through four reservoirs. Each one had its own personality.

Bukit Batok Quarry came first — still and glassy, a little eerie at that hour of the morning. The kind of place that feels like it's keeping a secret.

## The storm

Somewhere in the middle of all this, the sky had other plans.

CAT 1 struck. A full-on torrential downpour, the kind that soaks you to the bone within thirty seconds. There was no shelter, no waiting it out — just me, the rain, and the road ahead.

So I trudged on.

## Rifle Range Road

If there was a single stretch that tested me mentally, it was Rifle Range Road.

Five kilometres of empty, tree-lined road. No shops. No people. Three military bases barely visible behind the fences. Just you and the sound of your own footsteps.

It sounds peaceful. It wasn't. It was the kind of emptiness that makes your brain negotiate with you — *just stop, sit down, call a Grab*. But the end was out there somewhere, and that was enough to keep moving.

MacRitchie Reservoir came after, and it felt like a reward. The boardwalks, the water, the trees — it was exactly the reset I needed before the final push.

## The finish

I concluded the trip at Bishan-Ang Mo Kio Park.

To mark the occasion, I did a handstand. Because why not.

The whole thing took the better part of a day — west to east, reservoir to reservoir, through rain and empty roads and a very questionable number of oat sachets. But I made it.

If you're ever looking for a way to see Singapore differently, skip the MRT for a day. You'd be surprised what's out there when you're moving at walking pace.
