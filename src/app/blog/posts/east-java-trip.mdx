## How it all began

Earlier this May in 2026, I went off to Indonesia on my very first trip with the friends I made in Air Wing, OCS. They were my fellow instructors who took the batch of 143/26 — a batch that holds a special place in my heart, since they were my very first.

It was a wonderfully impromptu trip. Caleb settled the tickets, Rayson sorted out the insurance, and everyone else was just vibing along for the ride. No rigid itinerary, no overthinking — just a group of guys saying yes to something spontaneous.

## Our guide for five days

When we landed, we met up with our driver, who quickly became so much more than that — he was basically our tour guide for the next five days. He knew exactly where to take us and when, and made the whole experience feel effortless.

He navigated the winding mountain roads like it was second nature, brought us to local warungs where the food was incredible, and seemed genuinely happy to share his home with us. East Java through a local's eyes hits differently.

The sunrise at Mount Bromo was something else entirely. We woke up at 2am, drove up through pitch-black fog, and then watched the sky turn from deep purple to gold as the volcano emerged from the mist. No photo does it justice — but Caleb's Fujifilm came close.

## Life on the road

Honestly, the journey itself was about 90% road trips and sleeping in the van. But somehow, that was part of the charm. The long drives gave us time to talk, laugh, and just exist together away from the demands of work.

One of the highlights on the road was the jeep ride — something straight out of an action movie. We were flying through the volcanic sands around Bromo in open-top jeeps, and naturally someone decided that was the perfect moment to jump for a photo.

## Chasing waterfalls

Tumpak Sewu was probably the most physically demanding part of the trip — a steep hike down into a gorge to reach one of the most jaw-dropping waterfalls I've ever seen. Your legs are screaming the whole way down, and somehow worse on the way back up. Fully worth it.

## The unexpected wildlife

East Java also served up some characters we weren't expecting. The monkeys at the temple area had absolutely zero respect for personal space or belongings — someone lost a snack, someone else nearly lost their sunglasses. Classic travel chaos.

## A trip to remember

Overall, I'd say this was a trip to remember for a lifetime. Great people, stunning scenery, and the kind of spontaneous adventure you genuinely can't plan for. There's something about travelling with people you've gone through a tough experience with — it just hits different.

If you're ever thinking about East Java, stop thinking and just go.
