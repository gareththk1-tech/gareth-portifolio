## 10am. Saturday. Let's go.

No plans. No prep. Just a half-awake brain and a phone.

I woke up on a lazy Saturday morning, rolled over, and for whatever reason decided to call Zeon and Vernon. Within the hour we'd hatched a plan to hike a random mountain in Johor Bahru, booked an Airbnb, and were already figuring out what to pack.

That's the thing about trips like this — they only happen when you stop overthinking them.

## Woodlands checkpoint

We met up at Woodlands MRT and joined the river of people crossing the causeway into JB. There's always a particular energy at that checkpoint — everyone slightly buzzing, not quite sure what's on the other side but excited anyway.

## Lion dancers and lucky timing

As we stepped into the streets of JB, we stumbled onto something we weren't expecting — a massive congregation of lion dancers mid-performance. Drums, cymbals, the works. They were celebrating a Chinese festival and the whole street had come alive around it.

Pure luck. The kind of thing that makes an already good trip feel a bit charmed.

## Prata for dinner

We checked into the Airbnb, dropped our bags, and immediately went out looking for food. We landed on prata — cheap, good, and exactly right for the occasion. I don't think I've ever had prata that tasted worse when you're hungry and on a trip, and this was no exception.

## The mountain

Sunday. Time to actually do the thing we came for.

The hike was harder than any of us expected. Steep, uneven, and relentless. The terrain was proper jungle — the kind that doesn't care how fit you think you are.
